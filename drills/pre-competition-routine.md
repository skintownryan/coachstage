title: Pre-Competition Routine Workshop
stage: "4"
type: mental
time: 40 min
players: Any
equipment: Whiteboard or paper and pen
summary: A workshop where athletes build their own personalised pre-competition routine covering physical activation, mental focus, and confidence triggers. Treats psychological readiness as a trainable skill.
objective: Build an individualised pre-competition routine that athletes own, believe in, and can repeat consistently.
setup: Relaxed environment. Whiteboard for group work plus individual paper for each athlete.
instructions:
  - Discuss — recall your best ever performance. What were you thinking and feeling beforehand?
  - Identify 2–3 personal confidence triggers — a memory, a feeling, a word or phrase.
  - Design individual routines — physical activation sequence, focus phrase, final mental image.
  - Practise the routine together as a group.
  - Each athlete writes their routine on a card they keep.
coaching: Make it deeply personal — there is no universally correct routine. Facilitate and ask questions rather than prescribe. A routine only works if the athlete genuinely believes in it.
variations: Video athletes narrating their routine for later reflection. Revisit and refine routines together after each competition based on what worked.
