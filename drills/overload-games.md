title: Overload Game Series
stage: "4"
type: game
time: 35 min
players: 10–20
equipment: Goals, bibs, cones
summary: A series of small-sided games with deliberate numerical overloads that force specific tactical problems to be solved. Develops tactical intelligence, positional understanding, and decision-making under realistic pressure.
objective: Develop tactical intelligence and decision-making through structured overload scenarios that mirror real match problems.
setup: Standard small-sided area. Adjust team sizes to create specific overloads each round.
instructions:
  - Round 1 — 4v3 plus goalkeeper. Overload team must score. Underload team must defend and counter.
  - Round 2 — 3v3 plus two neutral wide players always with attacking team. Develops wide play.
  - Round 3 — equal numbers but one team must always have a player in an advanced position.
  - Debrief each round with athlete-led analysis before moving to the next.
coaching: Name the tactical problem clearly before each round. Let athletes attempt to solve it before intervening. Use the debrief to surface what they discovered not what you wanted them to learn.
variations: Change the overload ratio based on the specific tactical focus. Add a pressing trigger condition. Remove the goalkeeper to emphasise outfield decision-making.
