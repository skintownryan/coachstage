title: Hip and Knee Stability Circuit
stage: "3"
type: physical
time: 20 min
players: Any
equipment: Resistance bands optional
summary: A targeted circuit addressing hip and knee stability — the key injury prevention focus for athletes aged 13–15. Directly reduces ACL injury risk particularly important for female athletes during puberty.
objective: Develop hip abductor and knee stability strength to reduce ACL and overuse injury risk during the high-risk growth period.
setup: Mark 4 stations. Athletes work for 40 seconds at each station with 20 seconds to rotate.
instructions:
  - Station 1 — single leg Romanian deadlift. Balance on one leg, hinge forward keeping back flat.
  - Station 2 — lateral band walks. Band around ankles, stay low, step sideways maintaining tension.
  - Station 3 — glute bridge with hold. Feet flat, drive hips up, squeeze glutes hard, hold 2 seconds.
  - Station 4 — single leg squat to box. Lower slowly onto a box on one leg, stand back up with control.
coaching: Knee alignment is the key coaching point at every station — knee should track over the second toe at all times. Never let the knee collapse inward. Correct this immediately and firmly but positively.
variations: Add a resistance band above the knees on squats. Progress single leg work to unstable surfaces. Increase hold times on bridges.
