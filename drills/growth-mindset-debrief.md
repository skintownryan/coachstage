title: Growth Mindset Debrief
stage: "2"
type: mental
time: 10 min
players: Any
equipment: None
summary: A short structured conversation at the end of training that shifts focus from outcome to learning. Builds a growth mindset and helps athletes understand that effort and improvement matter more than winning.
objective: Build a growth mindset culture where effort, learning, and improvement are valued above outcomes.
setup: Bring athletes together in a relaxed group. Seated on the ground works well.
instructions:
  - Ask one athlete — what was hard today?
  - Ask another — what did you get better at?
  - Ask another — what would you do differently next time?
  - Finish with — what is one thing you are proud of from today?
  - Keep it to 3–4 minutes maximum.
coaching: Never answer your own questions. Wait for their responses. Validate every answer genuinely. Link improvement to effort — you got better because you kept trying.
variations: Use a talking object that gets passed around. Write answers on a whiteboard. Let athletes lead the debrief over time.
