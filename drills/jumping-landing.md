title: Jump and Land Safely
stage: "1"
type: physical
time: 15 min
players: Any
equipment: Cones, low soft obstacles optional
summary: Athletes practice different jumping and landing patterns focusing on soft controlled landings. Develops basic plyometric awareness, body control, and lays the foundation for safe movement patterns throughout their athletic career.
objective: Develop basic jumping mechanics and safe landing patterns that protect joints throughout an athletic career.
setup: Clear flat space. Optional low soft obstacles or floor markers.
instructions:
  - Two foot jump — jump up and land softly on two feet with bent knees. Stick the landing for 2 seconds.
  - Broad jump — jump forward as far as possible landing softly and controlled.
  - Hop and stick — jump off one foot land on the same foot and freeze.
  - Box jump — jump onto a low step or marker and land in a squat position.
  - All landings must be quiet — loud landings mean stiff knees, which is the injury risk.
coaching: The single coaching cue at this age is land quietly. Quiet landing equals bent knees which equals safe joints. Make it a game — who can land most silently?
variations: Add a direction change after landing. Jump over a partner lying on the ground. Progress to consecutive jumps with minimal ground contact time.
