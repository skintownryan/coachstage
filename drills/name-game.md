title: Name and Move Game
stage: "1"
type: mental
time: 10 min
players: 6–20
equipment: None
summary: Athletes learn each other's names through a passing game combined with movement. Builds team connection, listening skills, and a sense of belonging from the very first session.
objective: Build group cohesion, listening skills, and a sense of belonging in a new group.
setup: Athletes stand in a circle. Coach starts with a ball or bean bag.
instructions:
  - Coach says their own name and passes to someone across the circle.
  - That person says their name before catching.
  - Continue until everyone has been included.
  - Progress — say the name of the person you are throwing to.
  - Final version — add a movement or celebration after catching.
coaching: This is about connection not performance. Celebrate every name. Never let a child feel embarrassed for not knowing someone's name — it is new for everyone.
variations: Add two balls going simultaneously. Replace names with animals or superpowers. Time how fast the group can get the ball around everyone.
