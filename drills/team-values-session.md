title: Team Values Session
stage: "3"
type: mental
time: 30 min
players: Any
equipment: Paper, pens, wall space
summary: Athletes collectively identify and agree on the values that will define how their team operates. Creates genuine shared ownership of team culture rather than values imposed from above by a coach.
objective: Build genuine team ownership of culture and behaviour standards through collaborative values creation.
setup: Large wall space with paper. Split athletes into small groups of 3–4.
instructions:
  - Each group discusses and writes down 3 words that describe how they want to play and train together.
  - Groups share their words and explain their reasoning.
  - Whole group votes to select the 5 most important values from all suggestions.
  - Each value is defined in one specific behaviour — what does it look like in training?
  - Values are written up and displayed in the training environment.
coaching: Do not steer them toward the values you want. The process of deciding matters as much as the outcome. Values imposed by coaches are rules. Values chosen by athletes are culture.
variations: Revisit values monthly and ask whether the team is living them. Let athletes call out value violations constructively. Create a visual representation of the values together.
