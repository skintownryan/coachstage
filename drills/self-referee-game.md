summary: A full small-sided game where athletes make all refereeing decisions and resolve disputes without coach involvement. Develops autonomy, communication, game intelligence, and prepares athletes for adult sport.
objective: Develop athlete autonomy, conflict resolution, and self-regulation within a competitive environment.
setup: Standard small-sided setup. Coach establishes the ground rules once then watches without intervening in any disputes.
instructions:
  - Athletes play a normal competitive small-sided game.
  - All refereeing decisions are made by the players themselves.
  - Coach intervenes only for genuine safety concerns.
  - Game pauses every 10 minutes for an athlete-led tactical debrief — not coach-led.
  - Athletes chair the debrief and identify their own solutions.
coaching: Resist every coaching instinct to intervene in disputes. Observe patterns without comment. Use the athlete-led debriefs to reflect back what you observed without adding your own analysis first.
variations: Add a specific tactical condition. Award bonus points for particularly good decision-making moments. Record the debrief discussions and review as a group later.
