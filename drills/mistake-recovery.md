title: Mistake Recovery Protocol
stage: "3"
type: mental
time: 15 min
players: Any
equipment: None
summary: A structured session teaching athletes a deliberate three-step process for mentally recovering from errors quickly. Develops emotional regulation and competitive resilience.
objective: Build a reliable post-error mental reset routine that athletes can use automatically in competition.
setup: Circle or relaxed group format. Can be done seated or standing. No equipment needed.
instructions:
  - Discuss openly — what happens in your body when you make a mistake in a game?
  - Introduce the three-step reset — breathe out slowly, physical reset gesture such as clapping hands, set the next action clearly in your mind.
  - Pair athletes — one performs a skill, makes an intentional error, then practises the reset immediately.
  - Repeat until the reset starts to feel natural and automatic.
  - Debrief — how did it feel to reset deliberately?
coaching: Share your own experiences of handling mistakes openly. Normalise errors as a universal part of performance. This conversation has as much value as the exercise itself.
variations: Use real match footage of elite athletes resetting after errors. Role-play specific high-pressure scenarios from recent matches.
