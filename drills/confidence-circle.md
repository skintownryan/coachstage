title: Confidence Circle
stage: "2"
type: mental
time: 10 min
players: 6–20
equipment: None
summary: A simple team ritual where each athlete shares one thing they are good at or proud of. Builds self-confidence, positive group culture, and teaches athletes to recognise and articulate their own strengths.
objective: Build individual confidence and positive team culture through structured peer recognition and self-affirmation.
setup: Athletes stand in a circle. Coach models the activity first.
instructions:
  - Coach starts by sharing one thing they are genuinely good at as a coach.
  - Each athlete takes a turn sharing one thing they are good at in sport or in life.
  - After each share the whole group responds with a team cheer or gesture.
  - No negative comments or laughter are permitted — this is a safe space.
  - Finish with a group statement — we are getting better every day.
coaching: Model vulnerability first — share something real. Validate every contribution warmly. Protect any athlete who struggles to think of something by offering a genuine observation about them.
variations: Share something a teammate is good at instead. Write strengths on paper and post them on a wall. Revisit at the end of season to compare with early answers.
