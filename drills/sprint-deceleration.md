title: Sprint and Deceleration Intervals
stage: "2"
type: physical
time: 20 min
players: Any
equipment: Cones
summary: Short sprint efforts with controlled deceleration and full recovery. Develops speed mechanics, acceleration, and the often-neglected ability to stop quickly and safely.
objective: Develop acceleration, maximum speed, and controlled deceleration as a key injury prevention skill.
setup: Place cones at 0m, 15m, and 25m on a flat surface.
instructions:
  - Athletes start at 0m.
  - Sprint at full effort to the 15m cone.
  - Decelerate and stop under full control by 25m.
  - Walk back slowly — full recovery between efforts.
  - Complete 8–10 reps across 2 sets.
coaching: Watch for forward lean during acceleration — this is correct. Praise controlled stops as much as fast sprints. Deceleration is a skill and a major injury prevention tool.
variations: Flying starts from a jog. Reaction start on an audio signal. Partner races keeping it fun and competitive.
