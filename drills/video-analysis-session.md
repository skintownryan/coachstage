title: Individual Video Analysis Session
stage: "4"
type: mental
time: 30 min
players: Individual or small groups
equipment: Laptop or tablet, match footage
summary: Athletes review their own match footage with guided questions to develop self-analysis skills and tactical awareness. Builds the habit of independent reflection that separates developing athletes from elite ones.
objective: Develop self-analysis capability and tactical awareness through structured individual video review.
setup: Relaxed environment with a screen. Have specific footage clips identified in advance to avoid wasting time searching.
instructions:
  - Athlete watches a 3–4 minute clip of their own recent match performance.
  - Coach asks — what do you notice about your positioning here?
  - Athlete identifies one moment they are proud of and explains why.
  - Athlete identifies one moment they would do differently and explains the alternative.
  - Set one specific focus for the next training session based on the analysis.
coaching: Ask questions exclusively — never tell. If they miss something important ask a question that directs their attention there. The insight must come from the athlete or it will not stick.
variations: Compare two clips from different matches to identify patterns. Let athletes select their own clips for review. Build a personal highlight reel alongside the development clips.
