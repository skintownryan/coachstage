title: Nordic Hamstring Curls
stage: "3"
type: physical
time: 15 min
players: Any — pairs required
equipment: Mat or grass
summary: A partner-assisted eccentric hamstring exercise that is one of the most effective injury prevention tools available. Essential for reducing ACL and hamstring strain risk during rapid adolescent growth.
objective: Develop eccentric hamstring strength to significantly reduce injury risk during the high-risk puberty growth phase.
setup: Athletes kneel on a mat or soft grass. Partner kneels behind and holds the athlete's ankles firmly against the ground.
instructions:
  - From kneeling upright, athlete lowers their body forward as slowly as possible.
  - When they can no longer control the descent they catch themselves with their hands.
  - Push back up using hands then use hamstrings to pull back to upright.
  - Complete 3 sets of 5–8 reps with full rest between sets.
  - Add reps gradually as strength develops week by week.
coaching: Expect this to be hard immediately — that is correct and expected. The slow lowering phase is where all the benefit is. Never rush the descent. Progress reps slowly over weeks.
variations: Reduce range by elevating hands on a low box. Add a resistance band to assist the upward phase. Progress to unassisted over many weeks.
